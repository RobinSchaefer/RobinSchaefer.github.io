About me

I am a Computational Linguist with a background in Psycholinguistics and Data Analysis. While my research interests have become increasingly computational during the last years, my current interests mainly circle around two problems:

  • As a PhD student in the Applied CL Discourse Research Lab headed by Manfred Stede, I work on the computational recognition of argumentation structure in tweets. Specifically, I am focusing on English and German data using supervised and unsupervised machine learning methods.
  • Additionally, I am a member of the LARGA project. In this project, which is joint work with Benno Stein (Bauhaus University Weimar), we aim at modeling and implementing rhetorical strategies in argumentation.

Having a background in psycholinguistics, I previously worked on the cognitive processing of agreement attraction in German using the ERP technique. This work has been supervised by Titus von der Malsburg.
