About me

I am a Computational Linguist with a background in Psycholinguistics and Data Analysis. Currently, my research interests are distributed across two fields:

  • As a PhD student in the Applied CL Discourse Research Lab headed by Manfred Stede I work on the computational recognition of argumentation structure in tweets. Specifically, I am focusing on English and German data using supervised and unsupervised machine learning methods.
  • Additionally, I am a member of the Qurator project at Staatsbibliothek zu Berlin (state library of Berlin). In this project I focus on OCR post-correction.

Having a background in psycholinguistics, I previously worked on the cognitive processing of agreement attraction in German using the ERP technique.
