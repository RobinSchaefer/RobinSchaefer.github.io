About me

I am a PhD student in the Applied CL Discourse Research Lab headed by Manfred Stede. My current research interests lie in the computational processing of argumentation structure in texts. Specifically, I am focusing on English and German tweets using supervised and unsupervised machine learning methods.

Having a background in psycholinguistics, I previously worked on the cognitive processing of agreement attraction in German using the ERP technique.
